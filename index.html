Dissertation Research

    Corporate Social Responsibility and Financial Performance: the Moderating Role of Ownership Concentration


    Undergraduate Dissertation, June 2020


    This paper presents new evidence from China on research on corporate financial performance, adopting dynamic panel OLS and GMM to control for endogeneity. I study the relationship of corporate social responsibility (CSR) participation and financial performance and pay special attention to the moderating role of ownership concentration in this CSR-financial performance relationship. The empirical analysis employs a panel dataset composed of non-ST, non-financial companies in China A-share market, covering the period from 2014 to 2018. The regression results show that CSR participation and ownership concentration, respectively, have positive effects on financial performance. Besides, ownership concentration has a negative moderation effect on the CSR-financial performance relationship and the moderation effect is more significant in non-state-owned enterprises. I put forward suggestions for enterprises and regulators in emerging markets such as developing countries to make decisions and policies based on the relationship of CSR participation, ownership concentration and financial performance.



    Railway Investment and Economic Development: A Dynamic CGE Analysis for China


    May 2020


    I adopt a dynamic computable general equilibrium (CGE) model to study the impact of railway investment on China's economy and simulates country-wide impacts through direct impact driving forces, including land use, production expansion, cost reduction, productivity increase, substitution demand and induced demand. Based on the data from 2002 to 2017, the analysis results of this paper show that China's railway investment has a positive stimulus effect on the economy. In general, the economic effect of railway investment is mainly caused by demand and production expansion, while the contributions of cost reduction and productivity increase are relatively small. The negative impacts of land use and substitution effect are negligible.



    Estimation of Large Dimensional Covariance Matrices: Empirical Analysis Based on A-share Market


    May 2019


    I test the applicability of two methods for covariance matrix estimation in the Chinese market. The Kronecker product model factorizes and fetches the logarithm of the large-dimensional matrix, and introduces the Minimum Distance (MD) estimator and the Quasi-Maximum Likelihood Estimator (QMLE). The shrinkage estimation is ultimately aimed to minimize the loss function, and introduces the linear shrinkage estimator and the direct nonlinear shrinkage estimator. The empirical results show that the two kinds of estimation methods are superior to the traditional sample covariance matrix estimation, and have strong applicability in the Chinese A-share market. Each of the two methods has its own advantages: the effect of the shrinkage estimator is stable in the change of data pattern and arrangement while the Kronecker product model provides a new idea for the estimation of the parameter matrix in the dynamic model.



    Media Supervision and CSR Information Disclosure in the Context of New Media: An Analysis of Metal and Non-metal Manufacturing Industry


    Coauthored with Keyi Xia, May 2018


    This paper samples 130 major enterprises of metal and nonmetal manufacturing industry from 2009 to 2016. We use fixed-effect Tobit model to study the effect of media supervision on corporate social responsibility (CSR) disclosure and explore the influencing mechanism. We also use quantile regression test the heterogeneity of the effect that media supervision exerts. The results show that media supervision and CSR information disclosure level are positively correlated, and the higher the level of CSR information disclosure, the more obvious the effect of media supervision. We suggest that the effectiveness of media supervision requires the internal governance of enterprises, the guidance and support of the government and the further improvement of the media industry, so as to promote the transparency of CSR information and urge enterprises to take more social responsibility.
